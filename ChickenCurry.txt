1. First heat the oil on the cooking pan. 
2. Once heated put the cut onion pieces on the oil.
3. When the onions get reddish, add tejpata, garam masala and roshun (ginger) paste to the pan.
4. The add Holud, Marich and Dhoniya spices to the pan and mix them well by stiring. 
5. Add salt and again mix them well by stiring well.
6. Close the pan with lid and let it heat up.
7. Once the oil starts getting seperated from the spices add the chicken pieces and mix them well so the spices mix well with the chickens.
8. Lower the heat.
9. Then add the cut pieces of potatoes to the pan and let it heat up well. 
10. After sometime add half bowl of water to the pan, lower the heat and let it cook for 10 minutes.
11. After that turn off the heat.


- Take out the extra layers of the Chickens' pieces
- Wash the pieces with water
- Slit the Chicken pieces deep
- Pour oil in the cooking pan while the pan is heating up
- Add the cut onion pieces to the pan
- Move the on ions arond and wait till they get brownish
- Add red chilli powder, turmuric powder, corriander powder, garlic-ginger paste and little water
- Stir them well
- Add 2 spoons of salt
- Stir them at times 
- Let the spices dry up, let the water get seperated 
- After that add the chicken pieces to the pan
- Lower the heat 
- Stir the chickens well so that it doesn't get attached to the cooking pan
- Add the cut potatoe pieces to the cooking pan
- Stir them well to get the water out and prevent the chickens from getting attached to the surface of the pan
- Add lot of water to let the chicken pieces get submerged in the pan
- Increase the heat to 9
- After the water starts boiling and got halved than the amount originally added, taste the salt in it
- If the salt is less then add some more salt to taste it better
- Add garam masala and cumin powder to the pan
- Stir them well 
- Done
